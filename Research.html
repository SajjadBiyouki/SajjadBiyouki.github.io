Research Projects

Data-driven Fuzzy Modeling for Thyroid Function Diagnosis and Nodule Detection based on Radioisotope Images

The thyroid is one of the human glands the diseases of which have spread worldwide. The thyroid gland produces hormones whose main function is to balance the metabolism and cells activities. There exists various techniques for thyroid diseases diagnosis; including blood test, biopsy, and ultrasound images; however, radioisotope imaging is the most recent and sophisticated technique to aid specifying thyroid diseases more accurately. To highlight the importance of thyroid disorders, it has been shown that roughly 2-3 % of United States population suffer from either hyperthyroidism or hypothyroidism. This project proposes a data-driven system for diagnosing the most prevalent diseases of thyroid (thyroid function disorders and thyroid nodules) based on thyroid radioisotope images. In the segmentation part, a novel modified clustering algorithm, namely Adaptive Possibilistic Fuzzy C-mean (APFCM), is introduced and applied to segment the thyroid radioisotope images. The PFCM algorithm is the extended method of FCM and PCM whose performance relies on several arbitrary parameters. In this regard, this project proposes a new clustering algorithm with more adaptivity to develop a reliable system.

ANN Simulation Optimization for Supply Chain Problem under Uncertainty with Customer Satisfaction Analysis

Real-world supply chain management problems are somehow complex, whose optimization is computational expensive due to the uncertainty of its critical decision variables.Simulation optimization is commonly applied to determine the optimal variables of the imitated behaviour of the complicated systems.Due to the uncertain nature of real-world systems, it is worthy to take account the robustness of resulting solutions. In multi-product supply chain system, The uncertainty problem can be addressed by considering the safety stock levels. This approach is investigated in this study, proposing an applicable framework to define the decision and environmental variables, and evaluate their impact on system performance. The simulation process is expensive in terms of resource, time. Therefore, Artificial Neural Network metamodel is employed to learn the system indirectly. Consequently, The study proposes a robust ANN metamodel-based simulation optimization approach, based on Taguchi view, to optimize a multi-product and multi-objective supply chain problem.
